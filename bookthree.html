  • Rating: 8/10

The Bright Ages is a history of the so-called dark ages of history, starting in 321 CE and ending in 1321 CE. The book also does not limit itself to Europe, instead focusing on different areas in different chapters, from as far west as Scotland to as far East as Baghdad. This broad focus is both a benefit and a problem with the book. On one hand, it allows for a general overview of the period of history across such a wide area geographically and temporally. On the other hand, the broad focus means that the history that is gone over is rather general without much detail.

For being a history book, it is rather accessible in it's writing, and not overly academic. Although each chapter covers a different geographic area and time period, it also flows extremely well.
